Club Types Overview

Understanding club types helps in selection and when to use them.

Drivers

Longest club in the bag both distance wise and length of club. Loft is usually between 8-12 degrees. Used for tee shots on par 4s and 5s. Large oversized heads for forgiveness and speed. Priorities lean towards forgiveness over distance. Most drivers offer adjustability.

Fairway Woods

Fairway woods typically include 3, 5, and 7 wood with loft ranging from 13 to 23 degrees. Used from fairway or rough for longer shots or off the tee on short holes. Has a smaller head than driver. Priority leans toward forgiveness and ability to launch. Most fairway woods offer adjustability.

Hybrids

Hybrid is combination between a fairway wood and longer irons. Usually 2 through 6 and lofts range from 14 to 28. Good replacement for some fairway woods or long irons. Distance and control of an iron with forgiveness and launch of a fairway wood. Many hybrids offer adjustability.

Irons

Categorized into 3 areas.

  • Long Irons (2, 3, 4) range from 18-30 degrees. Depending on your skill level and swing most players no longer use these, instead use higher fairway woods or hybrids.
  • Mid irons (5, 6, 7) range from 24 to 36 degrees. They are mostly used for approach shots to the green. Forgiveness and descent angle are some important metrics to ensure your ball stays on the green.
  • Short irons (8, 9, P) range from 32 to 48 degrees. Considered your scoring clubs. Priority should be high ball flights and soft landings.

Wedges

Wedges are used for a variety of shots: full swings, pitch shots, chip shots, flop shots, and bunker shots. Usually range from 48 to 60 degrees. Ideally look for control, accuracy, and stopping ability.

Putter

Designed for use on the green but can also be used off the green. Distance control and ability to start your putt on the designated line are important.
