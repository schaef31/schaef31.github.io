Getting Fitted Recommendations

A professional fitting session is one of the best investments you can make in your golf game. It uses data from your swing to customize clubs (loft, lie, shaft flex, grip size) so they match your body, swing speed, and goals. This leads to better consistency, more distance, and fewer bad habits. Most fittings take 45–90 minutes and cost $50–$150 (often credited toward purchases).

Where to Get Fitted

  • Club Champion (clubchampion.com): Offers highly customized fittings with over 65,000 head/shaft combinations, and advanced launch monitors for precise data-driven recommendations.
  • Austad's (austads.com): A regional golf retailer with in-store fitting services using launch monitors, focusing on major brands, and personalized club adjustments.
  • Local golf shops or golf course pro shops often offer fittings, providing convenient, course-specific advice, and access to demo clubs from brands carried locally.

How to Prepare

  • Bring your current set of clubs (driver through putter) so they can compare to new clubs.
  • Wear your normal golf shoes and glove,fittings are done in real playing conditions.
  • Warm up when you get there and take breaks as needed, it is not a race.
  • Know your typical distances and any swing issues (slices, hooks, inconsistent contact).

What to Expect

  • Hit 5–10 shots with each club while a launch monitor tracks metrics (swing speed, launch angle, spin rate, etc.).
  • Fitter will test different shafts, heads, and lofts to find the best combination.
  • You'll see immediate feedback on screen (distance, dispersion, ball flight).
  • End with recommendations and possibly a custom order or adjustments to your current clubs.
